Next steps · Third-party & tag governance
Theme-side wins are in place; the next gains mostly come from a clean, single path for analytics and marketing tags.
⚠️
Duplicate measurement: When the same events are fired from both Shopify sales-channel apps and a tag-manager or data-layer setup, you risk inflated counts, conflicting attribution, and unnecessary script weight. Sorting that out improves both data quality and room for further speed work.
Area What to watch for Typical direction
Analytics & ad pixels Native app integrations and tag-manager tags both firing similar pixels or conversion events. For each platform, choose one implementation (app or tag manager) and disable the other after validation.
Tag containers More than one tag-manager container (or duplicate loader) on the same storefront. Confirm which container(s) should load in production; remove or merge anything unintentional.
Theme & legacy snippets Old embeds or hardcoded scripts that repeat what your data layer or apps already send. Audit theme app embeds and custom liquid; remove redundant pixels and test in preview before publishing.
Apps & widgets Loyalty, referrals, or reviews loading scripts that overlap tag-managed events. Check with vendors whether a single integration path is enough; turn off duplicates where safe.
Tag manager performance tips
Applies to Google Tag Manager and similar setups—useful alongside cleaning up duplicate pixels.
1
Remove old and unused tags Audit GTM for tags that are no longer serving active campaigns or tracking needs. Fewer tags = faster container load.
2
Defer marketing tags to Window Loaded (or time-based trigger) Move tags that fire on pageview to fire on Window Loaded instead. For non-critical tags, add a time-delay trigger (e.g. 2000ms). Note: later firing can reduce data capture rates — balance accuracy vs speed.
3
Remove unused visibility triggers Visibility triggers run a check every second to detect on-screen elements. Remove any that aren't critical — they can measurably drag down TBT.
Room to grow

The optimized theme has delivered solid LCP and payload reductions across Homepage, PLP, and PDP—with the largest LCP and payload moves on PDP (−696ms, −1.03MB) and PLP (−602ms). After tag and pixel duplication are tightened up, further TBT and score gains are realistic on all three templates—including addressing Homepage TBT, which ticked up in these runs.
